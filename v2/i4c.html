The CCCP (Cyber Crime Complaints Portal) module is a strategic differentiator for Clari5. It creates a closed loop between victim reporting, national intelligence, and bank-level action. Banks processing the most complaints are also handling the largest public trust — ensuring that fraud victims have their cases investigated and funds traced in real-time rather than waiting days or weeks for manual investigation. The total 1,37,607 complaints in a single month reflects the scale of India's digital fraud challenge and Clari5's role in addressing it.

Step 1: Victim Reports on NCCRP
National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal

A fraud victim visits cybercrime.gov.in and files a complaint. They provide: the amount lost, date/time of the transaction, their bank account details, and any information about the fraudulent account. NCCRP assigns a complaint ID and immediately routes it to I4C for investigation. The speed of this report is critical — the faster the victim reports, the higher the chance of fund recovery before the fraudster disperses the money.

Step 2: I4C Traces the Funds
National fraud database analysis

I4C's national database identifies the destination account of the fraud (Layer 1). It then follows the money trail — if those funds were transferred onward to other accounts, those become Layer 2, Layer 3 etc. Each layer has progressively lower recovery probability as funds move further from the original fraud.

Step 3: Real-time CCCP Alert to Clari5
Instant notification to participating banks

I4C sends a real-time freeze request to all banks where traced accounts exist — if the account is at PNB, Canara, or IDBI, Clari5's CCCP module receives the alert instantly. This replaces the old manual process where a police complaint would take days to reach the bank's fraud team. Real-time processing is the core value proposition.

Step 4: Layer 1 Account Freeze
First receiving account — highest priority

Layer 1 is the account that directly received the stolen funds from the victim. Freezing this account is the highest priority action. If the fraudster hasn't moved the money yet, a Layer 1 freeze can result in 100% fund recovery. Clari5 automates this freeze action upon receiving the I4C alert, eliminating human delay.

Step 5: Layer 2+ Downstream Freeze
Following the money trail

If funds have been transferred onward, Layer 2 accounts (the accounts that received money from Layer 1) are targeted next. With each additional layer, recovery probability decreases — by Layer 4-5, funds are typically dispersed into cash or crypto. This is why early intervention at Layer 1 is so critical.

Step 6: Fund Recovery
Return of stolen funds to victim

Once accounts are frozen, the legal and compliance process begins for fund reversal. The entire Clari5 CCCP process from complaint receipt to account freeze can happen in seconds — transforming what was a multi-day manual process into an automated, real-time response system. For victims, this speed is the difference between recovering their money and losing it permanently.

IDBI Bank — 52,343 Complaints
Largest I4C complaint volume in January 2026

IDBI processed the highest volume of I4C complaints — 52,343 in January 2026. This reflects both IDBI's large customer base and its comprehensive integration of the Clari5 CCCP module. High complaint volume correlates with greater public trust — customers knowing their fraud reports will trigger immediate action are more likely to report quickly.

PNB — 49,794 Complaints
Second highest I4C volume · Also a scenario growth leader

Punjab National Bank combines strong CCCP integration with one of the highest scenario counts in the ecosystem (288 scenarios, 55.2% prevention ratio). PNB represents a comprehensive fraud prevention strategy — both proactive prevention via scenarios and reactive response via I4C integration.

Canara Bank — 35,470 Complaints
Third highest I4C volume · Also top in amount saved

Canara Bank is the only bank appearing in three major rankings — I4C complaints, amount saved (₹5.4Cr), and highest alert volume (1.1M). This makes Canara the most comprehensively equipped bank in the India region for fraud management.
